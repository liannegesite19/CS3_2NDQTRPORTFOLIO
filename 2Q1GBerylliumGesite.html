> Turon !

Turon is a popular Filipino snack made from sliced bananas covered in a spring roll wrapper. It's deep-fried until the wrapper turns golden and crispy, and then coated with caramelized sugar, making every bite deliciously crunchy and sweet. It may also include other fillings such as langka, or more commonly known as jackfruit (my favorite !), sweet potato, mango, coconut and many more depending on the region. Turon is often sold by street vendors everywhere in the Philippines, whether it be at the market or at popular festivals. Many Filipino restuarants also serve it as a dessert, usually with a scoop of ice cream on the side to contrast the turon's hot, crispy texture with the cold, creamy ice cream, further enhancing it's flavor.

Any one of my friends can tell you that I've been absolutely in love with turon ever since I came out of the womb. At my old school, the overpriced turon was always my go-to snack during recess. Somehow, I never got tired of it, even after eating it everyday for a whole year straight. Until now, It's always my first choice when it comes to desserts or a simple snack to pick me up after a long week. What I love the most about turon is how every bite always has a satisfying crunch to it, followed by the hot, tender and sweet banana and the even sweeter langka filling (if there is any) and coat of caramelized sugar. Origin Originally, it came from Filipino communities living nearby crop fields and banana trees. Farmers who had extra banana produce would give these to the locals nearby, who came up with the snack and started selling it on the roadside.
Main Ingredients Saba Bananas, Brown Sugar, and Lumpia wrappers. Other common ingredients include: langka, chocolate, ube jam, magoes, coconuts, cheese, ham, sweet potato and many more.
Texture Sweet, crunchy and creamy. Best eaten hot.
